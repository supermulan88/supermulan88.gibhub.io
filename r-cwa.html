The Problem

Current field detectors force
an unacceptable trade-off.

Fast but non-specific, or specific but slow and logistics-heavy. Neither is acceptable when every second of CWA exposure matters.

🚫
Slow Confirmation

Portable GC-MS systems — the current gold standard for field confirmation — require 5–15 minutes per analysis cycle. In a CWA event, that delay means continued exposure. Speed is not a convenience — it is a life-safety requirement.

→r-CWA™ delivers sub-second screening with full confirmation in under 120 seconds — without sacrificing molecular specificity.
⛰️
Logistics Burden

GC-MS systems require compressed carrier gas cylinders, GC columns, calibration gases, and significant maintenance. These consumables add weight, create supply chain dependencies, and make the equipment difficult to deploy on UAVs, in remote environments, or by dismounted soldiers.

→r-CWA™ is tank-free. An onboard ambient air-scrubbing cartridge replaces compressed gases. The only consumable lasts hundreds of operational hours.
🔴
Binary Output

Most portable detectors produce a simple threshold alarm — positive or negative. They cannot quantify confidence, handle unknown compounds, or distinguish between overlapping agents and interferents. In high-stakes scenarios, a wrong answer is as dangerous as no answer.

→r-CWA™ outputs probabilistic confidence scores, functional group attribution for unknowns, and a full inference chain — not just a binary alarm.
📏
Blind Spots During Analysis

Traditional GC-based systems are effectively blind during their analysis cycle — air is not monitored while the column is processing. In a dynamic threat environment, a second release during the first analysis window goes undetected.

→Dual-fiber design ensures zero-blind-spot operation. One fiber monitors continuously while the second undergoes thermal purge.
🔌
Poor Selectivity in Mixtures

Standard Raman spectroscopy struggles with mixed samples — overlapping spectral peaks from multiple compounds create ambiguous signals that simple library-matching algorithms cannot reliably resolve, particularly at the trace concentrations relevant to CWA detection.

→Three-layer deconvolution — physical (HCPCF), temporal (fractional desorption), and digital (Bayesian AI) — resolves overlapping components without physical separation.
🛠️
Inflexible Against Novel Threats

Hardware-defined detection systems cannot respond to emerging synthetic agents or new chemical threats without physical hardware replacement. As adversarial chemistry evolves, the sensor fleet becomes obsolete.

→Software-defined detection. New Bayesian priors and spectral libraries are pushed as wireless updates — the hardware is future-proof against emerging threats.
Detection Architecture

Three layers of separation.
One integrated decision.

r-CWA™ overcomes Raman's traditional sensitivity and selectivity limitations through a compounding three-layer approach — each layer solving a specific failure mode of conventional systems.

Layer 1 — Physical

HCPCF Signal Enhancement

Hollow-Core Photonic Crystal Fiber confines both the laser beam and the gas sample within a micron-scale hollow core. The extended interaction path length — orders of magnitude greater than lens-based Raman — dramatically increases signal-to-noise ratio, enabling sub-ppb detection of target agents.

  • → 1,000× signal enhancement over standard Raman
  • → Sub-ppb detection sensitivity
  • → Fiber coiled to ~10 cm — fully portable
  • → Laser path fully enclosed — Class 1 safety profile
  • → Cartridge-style fiber module — 30-second swap in dirty environments
Layer 2 — Temporal

Fractional Thermal Desorption

A micro-thermal-trap pre-concentrates the sample stream in parallel with continuous monitoring. When analysis is triggered, a controlled temperature ramp desorbs compounds by volatility class — lighter interferents first, heavier threat agents later. This provides physical temporal separation without GC columns, carrier gases, or complex fluidics.

  • → Temperature ramp separates by volatility class
  • → Background interferents released before CWA agents
  • → No carrier gas — ambient air scrubbing only
  • → Provides 3D data cube: intensity × shift × temperature
  • → Pre-concentrator silently loads during screening mode
Layer 3 — Digital

Bayesian AI Deconvolution

A hybrid AI engine performs real-time spectral analysis. A 1D-CNN handles sub-second anomaly detection during continuous monitoring. Bayesian inference performs full agent identification during confirmation — providing probabilistic confidence scores, unknown compound attribution, and physics-constrained reasoning rather than black-box pattern matching.

  • → CNN for real-time 1σ/2σ anomaly triggering
  • → Bayesian inference for probabilistic identification
  • → Physics-informed priors — constrained by known Raman cross-sections
  • → Fuses spectral + thermal metadata for 3D deconvolution
  • → Cloud-updatable library — new threats added as software

🏴

The Adaptive Trigger — Context-Aware Sampling

When the CNN detects a 1σ spectral deviation — before any peak is identified — the system silently ramps the pump, increases laser integration time, and primes the pre-concentrator. This “pre-discovery” state maximises data collection during the critical window between “something is present” and “agent confirmed,” without triggering a false alarm. Confirmation is only declared at ≥4σ confidence. This approach doubles effective battery life compared to continuous high-force operation, while ensuring no trace event is missed.

⚙️ Physics-Informed Priors

Unlike black-box neural networks, the Bayesian model's priors are constrained by the known Raman vibrational cross-sections of each target agent. The model does not guess — it asks: "Given the known molecular bond frequencies of Sarin, VX, and HD, what combination of concentrations most likely produced this exact spectral signature?"

This approach provides confidence intervals rather than binary outputs, and reports high residual error when the spectrum does not fit any known agent — flagging the unknown without forcing a false match.

🔗 Cross-Domain Validation: BreathDX VOC Library

The Bayesian inference architecture was originally developed and validated for clinical VOC biomarker detection in exhaled breath — a demanding application requiring sub-ppb sensitivity and confident identification in complex biological matrices. The resulting human-background VOC spectral library directly reduces false positive rates in field CWA detection by enabling the AI to subtract “human-typical” signals from “threat-typical” signals. The methodology is proven; only the agent library changes.

❓ Handling Unknown Compounds — The Three-Layer Defense

When the AI encounters a signal that does not match any known agent, it does not force a false match or remain silent. It executes a hierarchical identification response:

1
Novelty Confidence ReportBayesian residual error is quantified and reported: "Unidentified organic detected — 97% confidence of novelty." High residual = the model is confident this is NOT a known agent.
2
Functional Group AttributionRaman peak clusters are mapped to molecular functional groups. Example: strong peaks at ~2900 cm¹ and ~1700 cm¹ → "Probable C=O (carbonyl) and C-H stretching. Possible organophosphate or ketone class."
3
Thermal Volatility ClassDesorption temperature at which the compound appeared narrows the molecular weight range. Late desorption at high temperature = heavy molecule. Combined with functional group, provides actionable class inference even without a library match.

☁️ Cloud-Updateable Threat Library

New Bayesian priors are deployable as wireless software updates — a “Digital Vaccine” approach. As novel synthetic agents emerge, updated spectral libraries are pushed to all deployed devices simultaneously. No hardware recalls. No field modifications. The sensor fleet responds to new threats at software speed.

Dual-Fiber Zero-Blind-Spot Design

Two independent fiber paths share one spectrometer via optical switch. One fiber monitors continuously while the second undergoes thermal purge. No detection gaps during cleaning cycles — critical in dynamic threat environments.

Compact solid-state trap collects and concentrates sample continuously in background. Temperature ramp desorption provides fractional separation by volatility class. Integrated heater serves dual purpose: desorption and fiber purge cycle.
